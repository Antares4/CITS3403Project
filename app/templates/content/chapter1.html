In western music notation, astave consists of 5 horrizontal lines and four spaces. Notes reside either on or inbetween those staves, representing the duriation and pitch of which should be interpreted by the performer.

Before a note can be placed however, some context must be established for a given stave.

A clef anchors the relative position of notes(determines which line/gap represent which note) and usually belong to two types: trebel or bass The following examples demonstrates the position of middle "C" in the respective clefs

trebel bass

The notes are represented cyclically useing the Alphabets [A-G]. That is, for each step up the stave, the note incriments alphabetically(decrement going down the stave). One step above G, the notation cycles, starting from A again but this time representing notes one octive above the last cycle(doubling its frequency).
