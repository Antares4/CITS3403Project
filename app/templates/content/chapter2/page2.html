Time Signiture

In music notation, a Bar represents a segment of time corrosponding to a spacific number of beats that are are closed off by vertical boundries called bar lines.

Similar to clefs, the Time signiture establishes the rhythmic context of a particular piece of music.

Time signitures consists of two figures positioned like a fraction symbol. The top figure represents the amount of beats present in a bar, whereas the bottom figure determines the note value of those beats.

The example above displays a 4/4 time signiture - also known as the common-time.
The number 4 above indicates 4 beats in a bar, whereas the bottom 4 represents the note value of crotchet.

Type of meter

We can describe a time signature to be Simple/Compound duple/triple/quadruple
The terms duple/triple/quadruple refers to the number of beats in a measure(bar).

Simple meter
The term Simple means that each of those beats can be broken down into two notes.
