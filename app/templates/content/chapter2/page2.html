Time Signature

In music notation, a Bar represents a segment of time corresponding to a specific number of beats that are closed off by vertical boundaries called bar lines.

This is a bar.

Similar to clefs, the Time signatures establishes the rhythmic context of a piece of music.

Time signatures consists of two figures positioned like a fraction symbol. The top figure represents the amount of beats present in a bar, whereas the bottom figure determines the note value of those beats.

This is an example of a 4/4 time signatures - also known as the common-time.
The number 4 above indicates that there are 4 beats in a bar, whereas the bottom 4 represents the note value of crotchet.
