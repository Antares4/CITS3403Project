CLEFS

In western music notation, notes reside in either one of 4 spaces or 5 horrizontal lines of a stave. But in order to place a note with it's intended meaning, some context must be established on a given stave.

A clef determines which note a particular line/space will represent. The trebel 𝄞 and bass 𝄢 clef were two of the most commonly used in music notation.

Click the buttons to find out the position of middle "C" in respective clefs.
