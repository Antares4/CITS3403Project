More representations

Infact, in between an octive, exists 12 notes, instead of the 7 we have just learnt.
This keybord demonstrates those 12 notes from F4 to E5.
Notice how there are 5 black notes in total, which fill the missing pieces of the puzzle.
Along with the 7 white notes, they form the 12 tone equal temperament - a musical system that used predominantly in western Music notation

  • f/4
  • f#/4
  • g/4
  • g#/4
  • a/4
  • a#/4
  • b/4
  • c/5
  • c#/5
  • d/5
  • d#/5
  • e/5

Accidentals

To represent the black notes, we need modifyers called the Accidentals.

The black notes were identified by modifying existing white notes with accidentals.
However, observe the keybord carefully, and you will notice that all of the black keys were juxtaposed by two white keys

This means that any black note can be named in two ways using its neighbouring white keys. To avoid ambiguity, two types of Accidentals - Sharp(#) and Flat(b) - were used to raise/lower a white key respectively.
