Infact between an octive, exists 12 instead of the 8 notes we have just learnt, as demonnstrated by this keybord.

  • f/4
  • f#/4
  • g/4
  • g#/4
  • a/4

  • c/5
  • c#/5
  • d/5
  • d#/5
  • e/5

The white notes, starting from F/4 to E/5 represents what we have drawn on the stave. To represent the black notes however, requires modifyers called the Accidentals.

Observe the keybord carefully, and you will notice that some white keys are right next to each other whereas some were divided by an black key. The difference between two concective keys is called a Semitone

There are two main types of Accidentals - Sharp(#) and Flat(b) that either rasies or lowers an note by a Semitone respectively
