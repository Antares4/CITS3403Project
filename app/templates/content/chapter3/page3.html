KEY signatures

Key

  1. To establish a key, we must indicate which group of notes(scale) we are after.

In some scenario this may require repetitive use of accidentals through out a piece of music which is redundent and more vulnerable to errors.

Below is a representation of the key B major without key signatures

  1. To solve this problem, Key signatures were used to represent keys.

Key signatures consists of a group of accidentals located before the time signatures of a piece of music.
The role of those accidentals are no longer modifying one note, but to modify all notes on that specific line/space.

The left passage is in the key of C Major where as the right passage is in A major
However the two notes shown are identical despite the left having an accidental.
