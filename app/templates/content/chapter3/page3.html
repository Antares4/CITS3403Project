KEY signiture

To establish a key, we must indicate which group of notes(scale) we are after.


In some scenario this may require a large amount of accidentals which is redundent and more vulnerable to errors


Below is a representation of the key B major without keysigniture

Therefore, to solve this problem, Key signitures were used to represent a given key

Key signitures consists of a gorup of accidentals located before the time signiture of a piece of music.
The role of those accidentals are no longer modifying one note, but to modify all notes on that spacific line/space

The left passage is in the key ofC Major where as the right passage is in A major
However the two notes shown are identical despite the left having an accidental
