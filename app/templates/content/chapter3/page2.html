What is a key

In music theory, a Key is a group of pitches that establishes the basis of a piece of music

  1. The main pitches used in a piece of music usually form a particular group called ascale
  2. The group features a center note called the Tonic and its 6 other corrosponding notes
  3. Keys are named after their tonic note and belong to either the major or minor category
